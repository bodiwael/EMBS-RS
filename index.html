Program Structure

The Six Phases

Each phase is deliberately structured to filter, train, challenge, and finally launch participants toward world-class research outcomes.

Phase 01
Announcement &
Recruitment
Program Launch ¬∑ Community Building
Stakeholder Onboarding

The opening phase establishes the public presence and operational backbone of the Research Program. This is when the program goes live to the world.

Program Announcement: Formal public launch of the Research Program across IEEE channels, academic networks, and social media platforms.
Call for Mentors: Recruitment of IEEE-affiliated and industry experts in biomedical engineering and bioinformatics research areas.
Speaker Acquisition: Identifying and confirming highly qualified speakers for the weekly webinar series running parallel throughout the program.
Sponsorship Drive: Securing institutional and industry sponsors to fund labs, materials, and competition entries for qualified participants.
Phase 02
Filtration &
Selection
Applications ¬∑ Assessment ¬∑ Interviews
Enrollment Decisions

A rigorous, multi-layer evaluation process designed to identify the most capable and committed candidates from the applicant pool ‚Äî determining their program pathway.

Application Review: Initial screening of submitted applications based on academic background, motivation, and relevant experience.
Technical Assessment: Domain-specific evaluations testing knowledge in biomedical engineering, programming, mathematics, and research fundamentals.
Structured Interviews: One-on-one or panel interviews assessing analytical thinking, research curiosity, and ability to execute a project independently.
Two Tracks Outcome: Top-qualifying applicants advance directly to Phase IV (Mentorship). All other accepted applicants proceed through the intensive training in Phase III.
Phase 03
Intensive
Training Program
3 Specialized Tracks ¬∑ Dashboard Access
Applied Projects ¬∑ Full Materials

An accelerated, hands-on training program covering core technologies essential to biomedical research. Participants are assigned to one of three specialized tracks via the program dashboard.

This phase is designed to bridge knowledge gaps and build practical research capabilities ‚Äî ensuring all participants are fully equipped before entering the research mentorship phase.

Track Assignment: Each applicant is assigned through the program dashboard to their best-matched track based on background and research interest.
Intensive Curriculum: Full structured materials, recorded lectures, references, and reading lists curated for biomedical research contexts.
Applied Projects: Track-specific hands-on projects to build mastery ‚Äî not just theory. Participants build, test, and present real outputs.
Qualified Applicants Bypass: Those who passed the Phase II top threshold skip directly to Phase IV ‚Äî their training is considered complete.
Phase 04
Mentorship &
Research Development
Weekly Meetings ¬∑ IEEE Mentors
Research Challenges ¬∑ Idea Formation

The heart of the program. Every participant is paired with a dedicated IEEE-affiliated mentor who is a recognized expert in the specific biomedical research domain relevant to the applicant's chosen direction.

IEEE EMBS E-JUST SBC serves as the overarching support system ‚Äî providing coordination, logistics, and guidance ‚Äî while each mentor focuses exclusively on tracking project progress and advancing the research quality.

Personal Mentor Assignment: Each participant is matched with a highly qualified IEEE expert based on research area alignment and availability.
Weekly Progress Meetings: Structured one-on-one sessions between participant and mentor to review work, discuss blockers, and sharpen direction.
Research Challenges: General open-ended challenges issued to participants to stimulate search for original research questions and define the project scope.
SBC Parallel Support: The chapter team assists with administrative guidance, resource access, motivation, and facilitates coordination between all parties.
Phase 05
Implementation
& Results
Simulation ¬∑ Hardware ¬∑ Lab Access
Materials Provision ¬∑ Result Generation

Once participants have achieved sufficient research progress and their project direction has been confirmed with their mentor, they transition into full implementation mode ‚Äî the hands-on execution of their research idea.

The chapter actively steps in during this phase to provide real institutional support, lab access, and the physical or computational resources required to finalize results.

Simulation-Based Projects: For computational, AI, CV, signal processing, or bioinformatics projects, participants utilize provided software environments, datasets, and compute resources.
Hardware Implementation: For electronics, BCI, or sensor-based projects, the chapter assists in sourcing components and securing lab access at partner institutions.
Lab & Materials Support: IEEE EMBS E-JUST SBC coordinates to provide physical labs, bench equipment, and experimental materials as needed.
Results Finalization: Structured process to generate, validate, and document scientific results that are ready for integration into an academic paper.
Phase 06
Paper Drafting
& Publication
IEEE Conferences ¬∑ International Competitions
Novel Contributions ¬∑ Global Recognition

With finalized results in hand, participants enter the final and most rewarding phase: transforming their research into a formally structured, publication-ready academic paper. All ideas across the program must be novel and original to qualify for IEEE conference proceedings.

Projects are tracked from early phases for competition eligibility. Those in competition-aligned areas are submitted to international competitions ‚Äî with the prospect of conference publication upon winning. All others are guided toward IEEE conference submissions with direct support from mentors and the chapter.

Paper Drafting Support: Participants receive guidance on structuring, writing, and formatting IEEE-compliant academic papers from their mentor and the chapter team.
Competition Pathway: Projects tracked for competition areas are submitted to BCI Award 2026, NHID Competition, MICCAI Challenges, or International Bioinformatics Competitions ‚Äî winners publish at the associated conference.
IEEE Conference Publication: Non-competition or non-winning projects are assisted by the chapter and mentors to target and submit to appropriate IEEE conference venues.
Novelty Requirement: Every project idea must be genuinely new and not previously published, ensuring all outputs make a real contribution to the scientific community.

People & Structure

Roles in the Program

Three distinct roles define the human architecture of this program ‚Äî each with a clear mandate and contribution boundary.

IEEE Expert

Research Mentors

Highly qualified IEEE-affiliated experts assigned one-to-one with participants. They hold weekly meetings, track project progress, recommend research directions, and provide expert critique. Their role is focused, not administrative.

Global

Speakers & Founders

International innovators and founders of novel biomedical technology applications. They lead the weekly Research Program Series webinars ‚Äî sharing frontier knowledge, real-world experience, and global research perspectives.

E-JUST SBC

IEEE EMBS SBC Team

The chapter team manages the entire program: recruitment, logistics, dashboard, lab coordination, materials, sponsor relations, competition submissions, and conference targeting. They serve as the central support structure for participants throughout all six phases.
